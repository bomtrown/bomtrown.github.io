Welcome to The Record Crate. What started as a simple idea to catalogue my favorite music quickly evolved into the interactive, physics-based visualization you see above. I wanted a way to explore my Spotify listening habits not just as a static list, but as a living, breathing ecosystem where tracks and artists gravitate towards each other based on their sonic footprint and shared vibes.

Under the hood, the Vibe Map is powered by a custom physics engine. Each node represents a track or album, injected dynamically via JavaScript into the DOM. The connections—those visual spring lines drawn onto the HTML canvas—act as physical constraints, pulling related genres together while pushing disparate sounds apart. It's a delicate balance of collision detection and spring forces, creating a web of music that reacts naturally when you click, drag, and interact with it.

Designing this space was all about merging the analog nostalgia of digging through physical records with modern, creative web development. The typography features bold, repetitive, squished text to ground the design, while the fluid nature of the node graph provides a stark, playful contrast. Feel free to toss the nodes around and see how the map settles—it's meant to be as tactile and exploratory as a real record crate.
